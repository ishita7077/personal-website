Guide

How it works

Each Haas practice session runs like the official interview: you complete a short device check, then answer 6 timed questions drawn from one of seven curated Haas question sets.

Within a set, the prompts are fixed so you can build real muscle memory, while different sets emphasize different themes such as career story, leadership, and school fit. Every question is mapped to a Haas category and a small set of bullet-point recommendations sourced from official Haas pages.

During each answer, your webcam records locally and speech-to-text generates a live transcript. After the session, the review screen shows your recording, a cleaned-up transcript, and the Haas recommendations for that exact question so you can compare what you said with what the school emphasizes.

What we are adding next

We are working on short, on-device summaries for individual answers and full sessions that highlight patterns in how you tell your story.

Over time, we will layer in a small set of targeted prompts—for example, to tighten a specific answer or connect it more clearly to Haas—while keeping all processing on your device.

Privacy

All processing happens in your browser. Video, audio, and transcripts never leave your device. Closing the tab clears all data unless you download or copy it.

Interview format

6 questions from one of seven sets (Haas Question Bank). For each: 45 seconds prep, up to 3 minutes to answer. You can submit early. In review, each answer shows your notes and Haas recommendations with official sources.

Troubleshooting

Camera not detected: Ensure no other app is using it. Try a different device from the dropdown.

Microphone not working: Check browser permissions. The level bar should move when you speak.

Transcription not appearing: Live transcription requires Chrome or Edge. On phones, offline transcription may be slower; for the full experience use a desktop browser.

Transcription failed for an answer: Your recording was still saved — use "Download recording" to keep it. For best results use Chrome or Edge on a desktop and ensure the page has finished loading the transcription model before you start.

Review screen — when does it say "Skipped" or "Skipped (no valid reviews)"?

  • Question card "Skipped": You did not submit a recording for that question (you left it out or ended the session before answering). No "not good enough" — it simply means no answer was recorded.
  • Session summary "Skipped (no valid reviews)": The overall summary needs at least one successful per-answer AI review. It shows this when every answered question either had no transcript, transcript quality too low for AI, or the per-answer review failed/timed out. So you see this when there isn’t enough usable data to summarize, not because your answers were "not good enough."
  • Per-answer "Transcript quality too low" / no AI insights: The transcript was empty, very short (under ~20 words), or flagged as corrupted/repetitive. That answer is not sent to the AI; you still see notes and can download the recording.

AI review or summary seems off: Add #ai-dev to the page URL (e.g. …/InterviewRoom/#ai-dev) and open Developer Tools → Console to see prompts and model responses for debugging.

Mobile: Recording and review work on phones and tablets. For live transcription and faster offline notes, use Chrome or Edge on a desktop.
