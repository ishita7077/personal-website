Then I found this Claude-based interview coach from Lenny’s Newsletter (I’ve followed the pod/newsletter for years). Noam Segal researched 30+ tech professionals, extracted what actually works, and encoded it into a plug-and-play Claude Code project.

Guide

How it works

Select a school to run a full practice: device check, waiting room, programme-specific timers, recording, and transcription. Most sessions use up to six prompts; Harvard uses five to mirror a typical file interview. Berkeley Haas follows the same flow as every other programme.

Open Resources anytime (top bar) to read the interview snapshot, official and community links, and a short planning guide—without starting a session.

After the session, review shows your recording, written answer text, school guidance for each prompt, and optional suggestions if you enable them.

What's new

Custom practice: Add your own questions and set prep/answer times. Same recording and transcription as programme practice.

Optional suggestions: Per-answer tips and a full-session summary when you choose to use them. Only your answer text is sent for that step.

What we're building next

More targeted prompts (e.g. tighten an answer, connect to school fit) and refinements based on your feedback.

Privacy

Recording, playback, and default text notes stay in your browser. Optional written suggestions send only answer text if you turn that on. Closing the tab clears local data unless you download or copy it.

Interview format

Programme practice: Prep and answer lengths follow each school’s research profile; structural prompts (video, Kira, team debrief, written reflection) may use different limits. Custom: Your own questions and timing. Review includes recordings, written answers, expected-answer guidance, and optional suggestions.

Keyboard shortcuts

Space — Skip current phase

Esc — End session and go to review

Troubleshooting

Camera not detected: Ensure no other app is using it. Try a different device from the dropdown.

Microphone not working: Check browser permissions. The level bar should move when you speak.

Transcription not appearing: Live transcription requires Chrome or Edge. On phones, offline transcription may be slower; for the full experience use a desktop browser.

Text notes failed for an answer: Your recording was still saved — use “Download recording” to keep it. For best results use Chrome or Edge on a desktop and wait until the page has finished preparing speech-to-text before you start.

Mobile: Recording and review work on phones and tablets. For live transcription and faster offline notes, use Chrome or Edge on a desktop.

Review screen labels

Question card "Skipped": No recording was submitted for that question. It does not mean the answer was "not good enough."

Session summary “Skipped (no valid reviews)”: There wasn’t enough usable text for a reliable summary—for example, every answer was blank, very short, or hit an error.

Per-answer “Text quality too low” / no suggestions: The text was empty, extremely short, or looked unusable. That answer isn’t sent for optional suggestions, but you still keep your recording and school guidance.
